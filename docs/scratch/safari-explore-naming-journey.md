# Naming Journey: Safari Explore

## What Is This Thing?

**Fundamentally:**

- Not an animal. Not a gathering. An _expedition_.
- A new category in the Grove skill ecosystem.
- The immersive, roleplay-driven wrapper for "systematically review and polish a collection of related items."

**What does it DO?**

- Turns tedious audit/review/polish work into an adventure
- Drives through N related items (curios, pages, components, API endpoints â€” whatever)
- At each stop: observe what exists, document findings, design improvements
- Produces a comprehensive expedition journal (the plan document)

**What emotion should it evoke?**

- Fun. Adventure. Discovery. The thrill of seeing something new through binoculars.
- The satisfying crunch of a jeep on savanna dirt.
- Sunset camp with a journal full of sketches and notes.

## Walking Through the Forest

I enter the grove. I see the animals already here...

Bloodhound tracking scents through the underbrush â€” but it scouts _code_, not collections.
Vulture circling the issue board â€” but it _cleans up_ dead issues, not _designs polish plans_.
Eagle soaring above for architecture â€” but it sees _systems_, not _item-by-item inventories_.
Panther stalking a single bug â€” surgical, one target, not a whole landscape.

None of them do what the user needs: _driving through a collection, stopping at each one, observing, documenting, designing._

I need something that feels like an expedition. A journey with many stops. Where the FUN is the journey itself.

I walk past the animals. Past the gatherings. I leave the forest entirely.

I'm in Africa now. Red dirt. Acacia trees. The golden light of late afternoon. A jeep rumbles beneath me. Binoculars hang from my neck. A leather journal is open on my lap.

This isn't a creature. It's an _experience_.
