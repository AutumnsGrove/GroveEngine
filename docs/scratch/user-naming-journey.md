# User Naming Journey

> What do we call the people who call the Grove home?

---

## The Problem

Right now we have:
- Users = Trees (established metaphor)
- But "trees" doesn't work as something you call people
- "Members" feels corporate, subscription-y, cold
- "Grovers" sounds like Sesame Street
- "Residents" lacks the nature connection

We need:
1. **Individual name** - What do you call ONE person?
2. **Collective name** - What do you call ALL of them together?
3. **Action/belonging language** - When someone subscribes, what happens? What ARE they now?

## What IS a User in Grove?

Not a customer. Not a subscriber. Not a member.

They're someone who **planted themselves here**.
They chose to put down roots.
They're growing in community with others.

**What they ARE:**
- A tree among trees
- Someone who planted
- Someone who's growing
- Someone rooted here

**What they DO:**
- They grow (write, create)
- They reach (connect with others)
- They shelter (provide value to readers)
- They root (commit to this place)

## Walking Through the Forest

I enter the grove. I see other treesâ€”other writers, other voices.

When I look around at everyone else who's here...

What do I call us?

Not a "userbase" or "community members."

## Candidate Directions

### Direction 1: The Rooted Ones
Focus on putting down roots, commitment to place.

- **Rooted** - "The Rooted" as collective, "I'm rooted at Grove"
- **Roots** - "Fellow roots" - but sounds weird
- **Planted** - "The Planted" - too past-tense?

### Direction 2: Growth & Living
Focus on being alive, growing.

- **Saplings** - New users are saplings, established ones are... what?
- **Growers** - "We're all growers here"
- **Growing** - "The Growing" (feels incomplete)

### Direction 3: Forest Community Words
Real terms for tree communities.

- **Stand** - A group of trees. "The Stand" for collective?
- **Grove** - Already used for the place itself
- **Canopy** - "Under the canopy" for everyone together?

### Direction 4: What Trees Do Together
How trees interact.

- **Interlaced** - Roots interlock, branches interweave
- **Kindred** - Trees of the same kind, related
- **Kin** - Simple, warm, familial

### Direction 5: Seasonal/Cyclical
Trees are defined by seasons.

- **Perennials** - We come back, we endure
- **Evergreens** - We stay, we persist
- **Deciduous** - No, this means we lose our leaves...

### Direction 6: The Forest Floor
What grows beneath and between.

- **Understory** - The smaller plants beneath the canopy
- **Groundcover** - Feels too small
- **Undergrowth** - Negative connotation

### Direction 7: Shelter & Home
Trees as homes, as places of safety.

- **Dwellers** - "Grove dwellers"
- **Denizens** - "Denizens of the grove" - too formal?
- **Keepers** - "Grovekeepers" - but that's more like admins

## Testing the Candidates

### "The Rooted"
> "Welcome, you're now rooted in the Grove."
> "Join 10,000 rooted writers."
> "A community of the rooted."

Feeling: Strong. Implies commitment, permanence, connection.
But: "Rooted" can mean "stuck" or "immobile."

### "Kindred"
> "Welcome, kindred."
> "10,000 kindred writers."
> "Find your kindred in the Grove."

Feeling: Warm, familial, suggests shared nature.
But: Doesn't have the tree/nature connection directly.

### "The Stand"
> "Welcome to the Stand."
> "Join the Stand."
> "You're part of the Stand now."

Feeling: Unified, strong, directly about trees.
But: Could sound militaristic. "Take a stand."

### "Evergreens"
> "Welcome, Evergreen."
> "10,000 Evergreens and growing."
> "You're an Evergreen now."

Feeling: Beautiful! Implies persistence, always present, enduring.
But: Already an existing tier name? Check this.

### "Kin"
> "Welcome, kin."
> "Grove kin."
> "You're kin now."

Feeling: Simple, warm, familial. Very natural.
But: Doesn't have tree connection explicitly.

### "Canopy"
> "Welcome to the Canopy."
> "You're under the Canopy now."
> "The Canopy grows."

Feeling: Collective, sheltering, overhead protection.
Individual: What's one person in a canopy? A leaf? A branch?

The community = **The Planted**? No, too passive.

When you join, you **put down roots**.
You become **rooted**.
Together, you're **the grove** itself.

Wait. What if it's simpler?

## The Simplest Answer

**The grove IS its trees.**
**The users ARE the grove.**

- Individual: "You're a tree in the grove" (metaphor, not title)
- Collective: **The Grove** - "Welcome to the Grove" is welcome to the community
- Action: "You've taken root" / "You're rooted now"

But we need a WORD for the people, not just the metaphor...

## More Candidates from Tree Biology

- **Cambium** - The living layer that makes trees grow (too obscure)
- **Heartwood** - Already taken for auth
- **Sapwood** - The living outer layer (interesting...)
- **Bark** - Protective outer layer (too rough sounding)
- **Crown** - The top of the tree, the foliage (individual focused)

### What about SEEDS?

Before you're a tree, you're a seed.
A new user is a **seed** that's been planted.
They grow into their tree.
Seeds become saplings become trees.

But calling established users "trees" still doesn't work as a title.

## What if we DON'T need a noun?

Some communities don't have a special noun for members.

Instead, they have:
- A verb for joining
- A state of belonging
- Language that implies without naming

Examples:
- "I blog at Grove" (action)
- "I'm rooted here" (state)
- "I planted my space" (past action)
- "Fellow grovekeeper" (role, but too admin-y)

I think the tiers are tree types:
- Seedling (free?)

## Current Best Candidates

### Individual (what you call ONE person):
1. **"A tree in the grove"** (metaphor, not title)
2. **"Keeper"** (one who tends their space)
3. **"Denizen"** (one who belongs here)

### Collective (what you call EVERYONE):
1. **"The Rooted"** - implies commitment, connection
2. **"The Grove"** - the people ARE the place
3. **"The Canopy"** - everyone together overhead
4. **"Kindred"** - familial, warm, related

### Action Language:
1. **"Take root"** - the act of joining
2. **"Plant yourself"** - the act of committing
3. **"Grow with us"** - ongoing belonging

## I Think I'm Circling Something

**The answer might be "Rooted":**

- Works as both state and identity
- Natural opposite: visitors (not rooted yet)
- Verb form: "take root"

**For collective:** "The Rooted"
